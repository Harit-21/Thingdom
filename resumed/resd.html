The Damn Great Resume Guide

This isn't a list of chores. It's a marketing document. Learn to build a resume that convinces a busy recruiter, in under 10 seconds, that you are the solution to their problem.

Core Philosophy: The Mindset

1. The One-Page Rule

Unless you have 10+ years of highly relevant experience, your resume is one page. Full stop. It forces clarity and values every word.

2. The Recruiter is Your Audience

They are busy. Your resume must be scannable, clean, and immediately communicate your value. They are not reading a novel.

3. Tailor Everything, Always

A generic resume is a dead resume. Tweak your resume for every job description. This is the single most important thing you can do.

Anatomy of a Killer Resume

Design & Formatting

  • ✓
    Font: Use a professional pair. A serif like Garamond for headers and a sans-serif like Calibri for body text is a classic, powerful choice.
  • ✓
    Whitespace: Don't cram everything. Use adequate margins and spacing to make the document easy to read and feel uncluttered.
  • ✓
    Consistency: Use the same formatting for dates, locations, and titles throughout. Consistency is a hallmark of professionalism.

The Final Polish

  • 1.
    Proofread. Then Proofread Again. A single typo can get your resume thrown out. Read it out loud. Use a grammar checker.
  • 2.
    Export to PDF. Always submit as a PDF to preserve formatting. Name it professionally: `FirstLast_Resume.pdf`.
  • 3.
    Get Feedback. A fresh pair of eyes will always catch something you missed. Ask a friend, mentor, or career services professional.

The Resume Generator: Bring it to Life

This is the Python script that builds the professional, one-page resume described in this guide. Simply copy the code, edit the resume_data dictionary with your own information, and run the script to generate your personal `.docx` file.

resume_generator.py

How to Use

1. Install Library: Open your terminal and run:

pip install python-docx

2. Save the Code: Save the script as a Python file, for example, resume_generator.py.

3. Edit Your Data: Open the file and fill in the resume_data section with your own details.

4. Run the Script: Open your terminal in the same folder and run:

python resume_generator.py
